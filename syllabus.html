Course Overview

This course provides engineering knowledge on air pollution, air quality monitoring and air pollution control strategies. It motivates students to maintain and improve environmental air quality and take appropriate actions to reduce air pollution.

Course Outcomes

  • CO1: Explain the sources of air pollution and different types of air pollutants.
  • CO2: Describe the effects of air pollutants on vegetation, animals, materials and human health.
  • CO3: Discuss different methods of ambient air quality monitoring systems.
  • CO4: Explain meteorological aspects affecting air pollutant dispersion.
  • CO5: Describe various air pollution control strategies.

Module I – Introduction

  • Components of environment
  • Definition of air pollution
  • History of air pollution episodes
  • Sources of air pollution
  • Industrial processes causing air pollution
  • Air pollutants
  • Types of air pollutants
  • Criteria pollutants

Module II – Effects of Air Pollution

  • Effects of air pollutants on health
  • Effects on vegetation and animals
  • Effects on materials and environment
  • Greenhouse effect
  • Indoor air pollution
  • Sources of indoor air pollutants
  • Effects of indoor air pollution

Module III – Air Pollutant Dispersion

  • Meteorological aspects of dispersion
  • Temperature and pressure relationships
  • Atmospheric stability
  • Temperature lapse rate
  • Atmospheric inversions
  • Plume dispersion theory
  • Gaussian plume model
  • Pasquill stability curves

Module IV – Air Quality Monitoring

  • Air quality monitoring
  • Ambient air sampling
  • Collection of gaseous pollutants
  • Collection of particulate pollutants
  • Ambient air quality standards
  • Emission inventory

Module V – Air Pollution Control

  • Control of air pollutants
  • Particulate emission control methods
  • Scrubbing
  • Cyclones
  • Filtration
  • Electrostatic precipitation
  • Gaseous emission control methods
  • Adsorption, absorption and thermal methods
